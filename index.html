Subject-by-subject study methods
Ext 1 Mathematics Extension 1
  • Touch Ext 1 every single day — even 20 minutes. The avoidance loop is the main problem, not ability.
  • Step 1: identify the exact failure point (not "vectors" — specifically "vector proofs" or "Cartesian conversion"). Open the textbook to that section only.
  • Step 2: trace 2–3 worked examples slowly. Write out every line. Do not skip steps.
  • Step 3: attempt 3–5 questions without notes under timed conditions.
  • Step 4: mark and write in the error log — for each mistake, record whether it was a concept gap, algebra error, or panic skip. Different causes need different fixes.
  • Differential equations: consolidate this topic alongside vectors. These two are the current priority.
  • Do not attempt long passive reading of theory. Questions first, theory only when stuck.
  • Current rank 14/25 → target rank 5–8. Every rank gained here moves the ATAR more than extra marks in already-strong subjects.
Eng English Advanced
  • Unseen texts: 5 min read → annotate techniques → 15–20 min write response → self-mark for technique coverage and conciseness. Do this at least twice a week.
  • Mod C (Father and Child / Spotty-Handed Villainesses): practice timed paragraphs. Focus on how gender and power is constructed, not just described.
  • Common Module (1984): build a bank of 8–10 flexible ideas about human nature that can pivot to any question angle. Avoid memorising essays — adapt ideas.
  • Module A (Richard III / Looking for Richard): build a comparative table — for each theme, list techniques and examples from both texts side by side. Practice writing comparative topic sentences.
  • Module B (Eliot poems): annotate 2 poems deeply each week — imagery, form, syntax. Practice writing about structure as meaning, not just imagery.
  • After each tutoring session, immediately rewrite one paragraph using the feedback received. Don't leave it 5 days.
Eco Economics
  • Already at rank 1. The gap between rank 1 and state-level territory is: fresher evidence, sharper evaluation, and question flexibility.
  • Maintain a live statistics sheet — update it weekly with new RBA data, ABS figures, or global indicators. This is the single biggest differentiator in high-scoring responses.
  • Each week: one timed full essay (35 min), one timed paragraph on a different topic (20 min), and one counter-argument or evaluation paragraph on a position already argued.
  • Integrate data inside the paragraph — not as an afterthought. Practice writing "as evidenced by [specific statistic, year, source]" naturally within argument sentences.
  • For policy questions: always cover effectiveness, limitations, and a comparative policy option. Three-lens evaluation is what pushes responses to band 6.
Math Mathematics Advanced
  • The teacher identified transcription and computation errors as the main issue. After each step, pause and check before moving to the next line.
  • Mixed past paper questions only — no topic-by-topic drills. The HSC paper is mixed; train for that format.
  • Work in 50-min blocks. After each block, mark every question. Every error gets a written reason: computation, setup, misread question.
  • Do not rework questions immediately after getting them wrong — close the paper, sleep on it, attempt again cold the next day.
  • Probability and distributions: spend extra time here if errors appear — this is a common late-course weak point.
Chem Chemistry
  • Teacher feedback: strong understanding, needs better stimulus interpretation. The fix is practice, not more theory.
  • For every stimulus question: read the stimulus twice before reading the question. Identify what concept it is testing. Then answer — don't over-engineer.
  • Acids/bases and equilibrium: write concise definitions from memory, then check accuracy. Precision in language is what separates band 5 and band 6 responses.
  • Past paper working scientifically questions: write full responses then compare wording against marking guidelines. Adopt the exact scientific terminology in the guidelines.
  • Error log: for every chemistry mistake, note whether it was a knowledge gap or a misread stimulus — these need different fixes.
Phys Physics
  • Teacher feedback: responses need more depth to access full marks. After writing a response, count explicitly how many marking criteria points are covered. If the question is worth 4 marks, there should be 4 distinct scorable points.
  • Circular motion and projectile motion: strong already. Maintain with 2–3 calculation questions per week.
  • Extended response writing: after completing a response, swap "the diagram shows" and vague phrases for precise scientific language. Read the marking guidelines and adopt their exact terminology.
  • Seek feedback on written responses — either from a teacher, from marking guidelines, or from a tutor. The report specifically mentioned this as the improvement path.
The psychology fix
  • The disheartening test result + avoidance loop is the actual problem. The content is learnable.
  • Daily contact rule: Ext 1 is touched every day — even 20 min counts. No exceptions.
  • The 10-minute rule: when avoiding, commit to 10 minutes only. Starting breaks the avoidance. Momentum builds after that.
  • The error log is the most important tool — it converts failure from demoralising into actionable. Every mistake becomes a target, not a verdict.

Session structure (every Ext 1 session)
  • Step 1 — Concept repair (15–20 min): Identify the exact sub-topic weakness. Not "vectors" — specifically "vector proofs" or "finding the angle between two vectors." Open textbook to that page only.
  • Step 2 — Worked examples (15 min): Trace 2–3 examples from solutions. Write every line. Don't skip the setup.
  • Step 3 — Timed questions (40–60 min): Past paper or textbook questions under time pressure. No notes. Stop when time is up even if incomplete.
  • Step 4 — Error log (15 min): For every wrong answer, write: (a) which concept failed, (b) where exactly the error appeared, (c) the correct method in own words. Never leave errors unmarked.

Anti-procrastination rules
  • Phone goes in a different room during every study block. Not face-down on the desk — different room.
  • Study in 50-min blocks with a 10-min break. Not 3-hour unfocused sessions.
  • When avoiding: commit to 10 minutes only. The momentum takes over after starting.
  • Grayscale mode on phone during study hours — reduces dopamine pull.
  • No passive revision counts as study: rereading notes, highlighting, watching explainer videos for hours — these do not build exam performance. Questions only.
  • Sunday 7pm planning session: write out the whole next week. Prevents decision fatigue causing drift on weekday evenings.
